Canadian Cuisine


Overview

A country pride on her multicultural diversity, Canada enjoys a wide-range of cuisines and draws inspirations from across the world. English and French settlements in the East Coast influenced earlier Canadian cuisines. This European influence combined with locally sourced ingredients formed some of the most unique and iconic Canadian cuisines, the most famous among them are poutine and maple syrup products. Starting from the 19th century, large-waves of immigrants from regions such as East and South Asia, Central and Eastern Europe, the Caribbean, India and the Middle East brought their share of creativeness to the Canadian cuisine. The West Coast of Canada enjoys a heavy influence from the Aboriginal People: from the Nanaimo bar named after the City of Nanaimo to the thousand-years of practices in preserving and cooking wild salmon. It is difficult to pin down what Canadian cuisine really is due to its diverse sources of origin. Then again, it is the amazingly flexible, creative, and adaptive nature of this multicultural country that gives Canada a unique flavour in taste.
